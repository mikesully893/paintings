My Story

Born in the west of Ireland, I’ve had an interest in art for as long as I can remember. Often doodling my way through classes in school, academic and social demands kept me from spending sufficient time practising my art skills. I studied nursing in university and worked for three years in hospitals in Galway, Dublin and London.

In 2020 amidst the global pandemic, I was diagnosed with cancer at the age of 26 which I subsequently received treatment for. I moved into a little white cottage in the countryside with my boyfriend and our cat Chai, who were my rocks during this difficult time.

Despite the circumstances of our return to Ireland, this little white cottage will always have a special place in our hearts, with many good memories formed here. This cottage, to me, is a place of healing and rejuvenation. After my diagnosis, the fragility of life became apparent to me and it enforced me to reflect on my life so far. It gave me the opportunity to discover who I am and what brings me joy in life. I wanted to ensure that I live my life doing things that make me happy and that I am passionate about.

The ‘Art Cottage’ was the place where I discovered my creative side. Creating art was my saviour and I found it to be very therapeutic in times of uncertainty. Painting and resin art are the mediums I enjoy working with. I am a huge animal lover so it is no surprise that I enjoy painting animals.

I hope that you enjoy my creations and that my story inspires you to discover your creative side too.
